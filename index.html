1. Plan Submission
Property owner/contractor submits plans to local authority.
2. Technical Review
Plans checked against Zoning, Fire, Health, and Building codes.
3. Adjustments & Fees
Revisions are finalized and application/impact fees are paid.
4. Permit Issuance
Legal document issued; defines scope, duration, and required inspections.
5. Construction & Inspections
Work begins with periodic checks to ensure code compliance.

2. Building Inspection Types

Type Stage Key Verification
Pre-Construction Before start Site plans, zoning, and site hazards.
Foundation Early stage Depth, reinforcement, and leveling.
Framing Shell complete Load-bearing elements and rough-ins.
MEP During construction Electrical, plumbing, and HVAC safety.
Final Before occupancy Overall compliance and fire safety.

2. Project Stakeholders

Every party involved has distinct legal and safety obligations.

Property Owners Initiate project, secure financing, and obtain necessary permits/approvals.
Contractors Manage daily operations, hire workers, and ensure work meets codes.
Architects Design functional buildings that meet all codes and prepare plans for approval.
Engineers (Structural/Civil/MEP) Provide specialized design expertise and certify integrity of systems.
Subcontractors Perform specialized tasks (HVAC, Plumbing) in compliance with specific codes.
Suppliers Ensure materials meet safety, quality, and design specifications.
